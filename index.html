Devathon Selected GitHub ↗ Demo ↗
Rainyway
Built because waterlogging data exists in India but never reaches drivers before they're already stuck in it. Surfaces live waterlogging zones on a routable map and re-suggests paths around flagged areas — selected at Devathon.
HTMLCSSJavaScriptTailwind CSS
Problem

Waterlogging during heavy rain is one of the most common, recurring disruptions on Indian roads — but the data on which roads are affected almost never reaches the person about to drive into one.

Approach
  • Map interface layered with live waterlogging zones over a routable street view
  • Route logic re-suggests paths around flagged areas instead of just warning after the fact
Outcome

Selected at Devathon — recognized for solving a real, recurring problem with a usable end-to-end flow, not just a UI mockup.

Research paper GitHub ↗ Demo ↗
AgriSmart
Started as research — co-authored at the 10th ICT4SD conference — then built out into a working dashboard. Gives farmers and regulators a single, role-based view of crop and field conditions instead of scattered data.
HTMLCSSJavaScriptDashboard System
Problem

Most agricultural dashboards target large operations. Smallholder farmers rarely get a single, legible view of crop and field conditions they can actually act on.

Approach
  • Role-based views so farmers and regulators see what's relevant to them, not one undifferentiated dashboard
  • Built on the research basis from a co-authored ICT4SD paper on e-agriculture, rather than starting from a blank UI brief
Outcome

A working dashboard that gives the research an actual interface — the kind of thing a reader of the paper could open and use.
